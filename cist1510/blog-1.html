Backend Web Development

Backend Web Development

Backend web development is becoming increasingly critical with the need for interactive websites making access to information more efficient alongside data processing, especially in the realm of e-commerce. Various popular services including Amazon, Netflix, and Google leverage artificial intelligence to advance initiatives.

For Google, their backend systems publish search results based upon a user’s location, interests, and a variety of factors related to websites, heavily emphasizing design. It’s not uncommon to see Google’s analytics service to be included in many websites and it’s often hard to stay away from them. Their systems work by using JavaScript to submit data to their services which is then stored inside of a database for view later by webmasters and analysis by the company – often leading up to targeted advertising.

With the power of backend web development being laid upon programming languages such as PHP, JavaScript (server-side, such through node.js), Java, Ruby, and more, it’s permitting shared hosting to thrive.

Just recently, cPanel increased pricing to not only be based upon the amount and type of servers it was installed on, but also now the number of users using a server. With their monopoly over the website control panel industry, it’s hard to escape and causing many businesses to raise prices. cPanel is an application which manages a web server’s software through backend programming languages and can even manage the languages themselves with the power of CloudLinux such through PHP and Node.js version selectors and even Python.

Evidently, it’s necessary for web developers to expand their skill sets and acquire some background in backend web development to become more competent in the industry.
